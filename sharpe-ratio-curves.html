  • Shape: Nearly straight line trending upward at approximately 45¬∞ angle
  • Smoothness: Minimal jaggedness - the smoother, the better
  • Consistency: Few and shallow drawdowns (temporary declines)
  • Angle: Steeper is better, but only if volatility remains low
  • Pattern: Steady, predictable growth rather than explosive gains followed by crashes

The ideal: A curve that looks like it was drawn with a ruler from bottom-left to top-right, with only minor deviations.

üìä Understanding the Visualization

Notice how as Sharpe ratio improves:

  • The equity curve becomes smoother and more linear
  • Drawdowns become smaller and less frequent
  • The path becomes more predictable and less erratic
  • The curve approaches a straight diagonal line

The Sharpe ratio essentially measures how straight and smooth your equity curve is relative to its upward slope.

  • Iron Condors typically produce choppy curves due to their payoff structure (many small wins, occasional large losses)
  • Daily options strategies often have lower Sharpe ratios due to transaction costs and gamma risk
  • A Sharpe > 2.0 is extremely rare in real trading and should be viewed skeptically
  • Consistency matters more than absolute returns - a steady 20% with low volatility beats a volatile 40%
